Battlefield™ 6

9.5
By Leonard on Oct 25, 2025

I've gotta say, Battlefieldâ„¢ 6 is exactly what fans have been waiting for. It's pure chaos in the best way possible - think massive explosions ripping through buildings while 128 players duke it out with everything from rifles to tanks. I spent hours just messing around with the destruction system, and every match tells its own story.

Combat and Multiplayer

Let me tell you about the guns - they feel amazing. Each weapon has real weight to it, and you'll definitely feel that kick when you're trying to control your shots. But the real fun? That's when you hop into a tank or jump into the cockpit of a jet. I had this incredible moment where our squad's helicopter pilot swooped in to save our pinned-down team, all while dodging missiles and weaving between collapsing buildings. The best part is how the modes scale - you can jump into quick 32-player matches, or go all-out in those massive 128-player battles where every capture point tells its own story.

The Good

You know what really blew me away? The sheer scale of everything. These maps aren't just big - they're living, breathing battlegrounds where anything can happen. One minute you're defending a point, the next a tank bursts through a wall you thought was safe. The way everything works together - infantry, vehicles, destruction - creates these incredible "only in Battlefield" moments that had me spamming the capture button to share with friends.

  • Massive, destructible large-scale maps
  • Balanced combined-arms gameplay (infantry + vehicles)
  • High-fidelity audio and visual presentation
  • Modes that support both casual and competitive play

The Bad

Look, I've got to be honest - there are some rough edges here. The matchmaking can be a bit of a mess right now, sometimes throwing you into seriously unbalanced matches. And don't get me started on the progression system - it feels like they're pushing you towards microtransactions a bit too hard. If you're someone who prefers running and gunning on foot, you might get frustrated with how dominant vehicles can be on some maps. I've had matches where it felt impossible to move between objectives without getting blown up.

  • Matchmaking and server stability issues at launch
  • Progression systems can feel grindy
  • Occasional balance issues between vehicles and infantry

Conclusion

Here's the bottom line: if you've been waiting for Battlefield to return to its glory days, this is it. Sure, there are some bumps along the way, but when everything clicks - and it often does - there's nothing else quite like it. The chaos, the teamwork, those "did that really just happen?" moments... it's all here. DICE is actively patching things up, and I can't wait to see how the game evolves. If you're into massive-scale warfare where anything can happen, you need to play this game. Just maybe give it a few weeks for them to iron out the server issues.
