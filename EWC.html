Esports World Cup 2026: 21 Returning Titles Announced

The Esports World Cup (EWC) is set to make a grand return to Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, from July 6 to August 23, 2026, promising an exhilarating showcase of competitive gaming. Building on the success of its inaugural event in 2023, the EWC is poised to captivate audiences worldwide with its impressive lineup of returning titles and thrilling competitions.

In a recent announcement, the EWC organizers revealed that 21 titles from the previous edition will be making a comeback for the 2026 event. This decision reflects the immense popularity and competitive spirit that these games brought to the forefront during the last tournament. Fans can look forward to witnessing intense battles and strategic gameplay across a diverse range of genres.

Among the returning titles are fan-favorites such as "League of Legends," "Dota 2," "Counter-Strike: Global Offensive," and "Fortnite." These games have consistently demonstrated their ability to draw massive audiences and foster vibrant esports communities. The inclusion of these titles ensures that the EWC will continue to be a premier destination for esports enthusiasts.

The EWC 2026 is not just about the games, it's also about the players and teams that will compete on the grand stage. Top tier esports organizations from around the globe are expected to send their best rosters to vie for glory and substantial prize pools. The event promises to be a melting pot of talent, strategy, and high-stakes competition.



EWC 2025 RECAP

The 2025 Esports World Cup, held in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, was a landmark event that showcased the pinnacle of competitive gaming. With a diverse lineup of games and top-tier talent from around the world, the tournament captivated audiences and set new standards for esports competitions.
