Hollow Knight: Silksong

A stunning sequel that expands on a masterpiece in every way.

9.5
By Leonard on Oct 25, 2025

It's been a long wait, but Team Cherry has done it again. Hollow Knight: Silksong isn't just more Hollow Knight; it's a new, vibrant, and challenging adventure that builds upon its predecessor's rock-solid foundation to create something truly special.

A New Kingdom to Explore

You play as Hornet, princess-protector of Hallownest, now captured and taken to the unfamiliar and cursed kingdom of Pharloom. The core gameplay loop remains: explore, fight, and uncover secrets. But Hornet controls with a speed and acrobatic grace that feels distinct from the Knight. Her silk-based abilities open up new traversal and combat possibilities, making the simple act of moving through the world a joy.

The Good

Where to even begin? The world is massive, varied, and packed with secrets. The new crafting system, using shell shards to create tools and items, adds a welcome layer of strategy. The soundtrack is, once again, an orchestral masterpiece by Christopher Larkin.

  • Incredible world design and exploration
  • Fast, fluid, and challenging combat
  • Stunning art direction and music
  • Vast amount of content for the price

The Bad

If there are any complaints, they are minor. The difficulty, like the original, can be punishing. Some late-game bosses may test your patience to its absolute limit. The sheer scale can also be daunting, with some players potentially feeling overwhelmed by the map's size.

  • Brutal difficulty spikes at times
  • Can be easy to get lost (a pro for some!)

Conclusion

Hollow Knight: Silksong is a triumphant success. It's a game that respects its players' intelligence and skill, offering a deep, rewarding experience that's rare to find. It's an essential play for any fan of the Metroidvania genre and a strong contender for game of the year. A masterpiece.
